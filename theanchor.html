THE ANCHOR

About the Project

The Anchor is a fictional seafood restaurant located in Key West, Florida. I created Branding and Identity, Merchandise, Marketing and Menu designs for the business. The objective was to develop a re-brand or initial branding reminiscent of what either an existing or brand new restaurant would undergo.

ROLE

Design

CLIENT

The Anchor

DATE

Feb. 2022

TOOLS

InDesign, Illustrator, Photoshop

Visual Identity & Mood Board

As a seafood restaurant, it was important to incorporate an oceanic and nautical feel into the overall brand. Sailing elements were an inspiration to represent the open sea, where the fresh ingredients are sourced from. I wanted to create an inviting and classy aesthetic, yet avoid the overly-expensive or intimidating vibe that often comes with the up-scale cuisine. The eatery was intended to serve both the local community and tourists of all ages and backgrounds, so ensuring the designs appealed to everyone was key.

Typography

Supporting the ocean theme, typefaces similar to those used on water vessels were selected. The sans serif "SF Pro" was particularly chosen for its simplicity and legibility, demonstrated with the smaller font size of the menu-item descriptions below.

To represent the wood, rust, rope, water, and steel associated with the sea, I selected a color scheme that epitomizes the ocean and its seafarers.

Logo

The cartoon-like logo uses recognizable elements of an anchor and rope, while the logotype fits the boat-naming convention and brings repetition and unity with the "O" acting as another loop for the rope to go through. All the elements are backed by a light-blue circular layer to round out the 3-D logo.

Mascot

Mascots are generally meant to be playful and charismatic. To align with the cuisine, I decided that a cartoon shrimp would work perfectly. Large round eyes, curious eyebrows, and a gentle smile make the character a friendly representation for the brand.

T-Shirts

Merchandise featuring either the logo or mascot provide a great way for patrons to support the business and advertise to the public in style. The design is simple, yet easily noticeable, with the mascot/logomark and logotype covering the full-width of the upper-back. On the front, the mascot/logomark grace the left chest region, giving a more subtle representation of the brand.

Business Card

The business card was designed to be sleek and simple. The steel color gives a smooth metallic look, while the tan rope acts as a border and divider to separate the logo from text. The text content is right-aligned, with font size and color used for visual hierarchy. The overall design is clean and on-point with the brand.

Menu

The menu is meant to look similar to a chalk-board with white food icons created in Illustrator and strategically placed, in addition to mainly white text against the gray background. Blue headers and tan sub-headers in a different typeface from the descriptions provide visual hierarchy. Blue waves are used as theme-based header underlines, while a rust-colored border creates a sharp inner-glow. Admittedly content-rich, white-space and distinct separation elements result in a functional menu.
