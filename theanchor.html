The Anchor

The Anchor is a fictional seafood restaurant located in Key West, Florida. I created Branding and Identity, Merchandise, Marketing and Menu designs for the business.

Visual Identity

As a seafood restaurant, it was important to incorporate an oceanic and nautical feel into the overall brand. Sailing elements were an inspiration to represent the open sea, where the fresh ingredients are sourced from. I wanted to create an inviting and classy aesthetic, yet avoid the overly-expensive or intimidating vibe that often comes with the up-scale cuisine. The eatery was intended to serve both the local community and tourists of all ages, so ensuring the designs appealed to everyone was key.

Typography

Supporting the ocean theme, typefaces similar to those used on water vessels were selected. The sans serif "SF Pro" was particularly chosen for its simplicity and legibility, demonstrated with the smaller font size of the menu-item descriptions below.

Logo

The logo use recognizable elements of an anchor and rope. Meanwhile, the logotype fits the boat-naming convention, which brings repetition and unity with the "O" acting as another loop for the rope to go through. I replaced the anchor and rope with a mascot in one of the logo variations. Mascots are generally meant to be playful and charismatic and to align with the cuisine, I decided on a cartoon shrimp. Large round eyes, curious eyebrows, and a gentle smile make the character a friendly representation for the brand.

Business Card

The business card was designed to be sleek and simple. A steel color gives a smooth metallic look, while the tan rope acts as a divider to separate the logo from the text. The text content is right-aligned, with font size and color used for visual hierarchy.

Menu

The menu is meant to look similar to a chalk-board with white food icons strategically placed, in addition to mainly white text against the gray background. To provide visual hierarchy, blue headers and tan sub-headers are written in different typefaces from the descriptions. Blue waves are used as theme-based header underlines, while a rust-colored border creates a sharp inner-glow.
