MENTAL HEALTH SUGGESTIONS

About the Project

Mental Health Suggestions are healthy habits that can be incorporated into an individual's day-to-day life to specifically combat the mental health disorder, depression. For a college project, I designed a full-color infographic meant to educate the public about possible ways to overcome this mental obstacle. The infographic was required to be created in Illustrator and include icons from the free open-source database, Noun Project. The objective was to design an appropriate and well-executed infographic that could be displayed in a proper setting such as a medical clinic, hospital, etc. Given the serious and sensitive subject-matter, it was especially important to be cognizant of how the infographic would be perceived by the audience.

Visual Identity

Depression is something that can have a major impact on a person's life. Its severity depends on the individual, but understanding the urgency of addressing it before it worsens cannot be understated. In a setting where patients are looking for advice or remedies for a medical condition, visuals like infographics should bring about a comfortable feeling. The design needed to be informative, while incorporating encouragement and having a calming visual appeal.

Typography

Being careful not to interfere or foster any unintended feelings from the viewer, I selected a general and basic typeface to display the text content. Helvetica Nueu is a simple, easily legible, and an overall neutral typeface that allows the other design elements to carry more meaning.

Soft and light colors were selected to portray a sense of calm, with different blues pertaining to openness and freedom to breathe. Using colors that alleviate the feeling of pressure and instead exude something clean, fresh, and new, was the intention.

Vector Art (Resource - Noun Project)

Being displayed in a single color, I wanted the icons to be as recognizable as possible. Without color to help distinguish different icon elements, the viewer should not have have to struggle to identify the visuals. Excluding the head icon, all contain similar stroke weight, thereby creating consistency throughout the infographic. The head icon is used differently in the design making its stroke weight here irrelevant.

Infographic

The infographic was designed to have a calming influence on the viewer, with a concept that was straightforward and easy to comprehend. By using thought bubbles paired with recognizable icons, there is a rudimentary element that posters and infographics often portray in a medical setting. I was careful to use rounded shapes such as clouds and circles to further exemplify this. Direction and separation between clouds is developed through paths created from gradated circles.
