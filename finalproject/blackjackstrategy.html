BLACKJACK STRATEGY

About the Project

Blackjack Strategy is a single-page web-application that can be used via desktop or mobile device to practice playing Blackjack. The application follows mathmematically accepted book/chart rules that determine the proper decision to make based on the dealer face-card and hand you are dealt as the player. As a personal project, I created the Logo, Layout, Design, and Development of the application. Pre-production included a mood board, color scheme, wireframe, and high-fidelity prototype, with production consisting of the overall page design and functionality. The objective was to design an application that was simple, educational, and fun, making users want to continue interacting with it.

Visual Identity

To bring the casino-like look to the user, creating a design with standard casino cards and table colors was key. Blackjack is a fast game with only four moves to choose from, so an emphasis needed to be placed on simplicity and providing a playing experience without unecessary distractions. Given that there is always one "correct" move to make for a player to have the best probability of winning a hand, it was important to clearly incorporate what that "correct" move is, into the design.

User Experience

Visual Design

Even though Blackjack is a straightforward game, "book-rules" that give a player the best chance of winning slightly differ depending on the number of decks being played. To account for this, I created a button for each game-type. This allows the user to have the option of playing either and developing skills for both. The game of Blackjack is played against the dealer, so the interface was designed to clearly depict two sides: the competition across the table, and the player. The four buttons reading "hit", "stand", "double", and "split" are nicely labelled and include icons that demonstrate the associated hand motion that should be made while sitting at the table. Once one of those four buttons are selected, an informative message appears, letting the user know if their selection was correct or not, and why. This design element was imperative as it gives users knowledge that they can use moving forward. Simultaneously, a "New Hand" button appears, giving the user a convenient way to play another hand. The overall design applies the less-is-more idea, purposely using very few elements, thereby making each element that I did include have a purpose. The color scheme and card icons are true to those typically seen in casinos, adding familiarity and continuity to the app interface. The limited navigation leads to simple decision-making and a rewarding experience. The application successfully operates as an enjoyable gaming and learning experience.
