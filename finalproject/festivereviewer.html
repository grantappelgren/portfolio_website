FESTIVE REVIEWER

About the Project

Festive Reviewer is a blog website where I share my experiences attending different music festivals. This website was a college project created with the goal of focusing on and giving an in-depth look into a passion of mine. The intended audience is any festival-goer interested in learning information, pros, cons, etc. about these events. Pre-production was required to include a color scheme, typography and wireframes, while production needed to consist of logo and responsive page design that ultimately resulted in a functional website containing multimedia, text, and interactive elements.

Visual Identity

Music Festivals are loud, bright, and often times chaotic. That said, I wanted to design a very straightforward interface that did not interfere with the subject-matter. Blogs should have the ability to be added upon, so a format that would be able to easily adjust as new content is added was important. Simple navigation and a consistent way of displaying my reviews of each festival across pages would help users compare festivals to one another.

Typography

Michroma's short and wide square-like appearance gives it a futuristic look, making it perfect for titles and headers. It works well with Montserrat Light, which is a very thin and delicate typeface used for the website's body. The latter is extremely easy on the eyes, a necessity for pages with a great deal of text.

Colors were selected to take a back seat to the content allowing users to read text and view images without distraction. Neutral light gray and dark blue make up the majority of text and white space, while tints of green and orange are used to semantically represent past (green) and future (orange) festivals as well as their strong (green) and poor (orange) grades. Dark red is used sparingly for a gradient effect.

Logo

I wanted to demonstrate creativity with the website logo by incorporating multi-purpose icons that doubled as text. Beginning with the "F", when connecting a circle to the left side of its base, the letter itself becomes the stem and flags of a sixteenth note. Having a music note within the logo for a music festival review blog is fitting. Likewise, the circle portion of the sixteenth note acts as the head of a person with the "V" doubling as the their collar. In this case, the person is me, the reviewer/blogger.

Landing Page Wireframes

A design ranging between 4, 2, and 1-column depending on the user's screen size creates an organized look-and-feel. A slideshow of festival images graces the top of the landing page, immediately offering users a glimpse into the festival atmospheres. Beneath this lies a brief "Welcome!" message explaining the purpose of the website. Festivals are listed as buttons, providing a way for users to interact with the site and access individual festival pages. On each of these pages, the same four columns (Production, Variety, Atmosphere, Return?) are featured and assigned letter or Yes/No grades, developing a way to rank festivals by section. Words can only do so much when it comes to describing something as sense-activating as music festivals, so supplementary images are placed throughout to visually enhance festival pages. Simple nav-bar and footer layouts with links and clickable icons border the top and bottom of each page.

Complete Landing Page & Website Demo

The final website successfully brings the wireframes to life. Minor changes from the wireframes to the website include a left-aligned nav-bar and minimalistic footer containing three centered and evenly-spaced contact links. Overall, the website is clean, inviting, and a nice place for festival-goers to visit. As an active blog, the design execution enables future content to be added in a fluid way.
