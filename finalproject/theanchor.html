THE ANCHOR

About the Project

The Anchor is a fictional seafood restaurant located in Key West, Florida. For a college project, I was tasked with creating Branding and Identity, Merchandise, Marketing and Menu designs for the business. Pre-production was required to include a mood board and color scheme, with production needing to include logo and logotype, mascot, t-shirt, business card and menu designs. The objective was to develop a re-brand or initial branding reminiscent of what either an existing or brand new restaurant and all of its elements would undergo.

Visual Identity

As a seafood restaurant, it was important to incorporate an oceanic and nautical feel into the overall branding. I wanted to create an inviting and classy look-and-feel, avoiding the super-expensive or intimidating vibe that often comes with the up-scale cuisine. The eatery was intended to serve both the local community and tourists of all ages, so ensuring the designs appealed to everyone was key as well.

Visual Design

To represent the water, rust, wood, rope and steel associated with the sea, I selected a color scheme that epitomizes the ocean and its seafairers. The cartoon-like logo and mascot reflect the idea of a friendly atmosphere that's open to people of all ages. I wanted to use simple and relevant imagery for the logo and mascot to be easily recognizable and properly highlight what type of cuisine is served at the restaurant. Merchandise featuring either the logo or mascot provide a great way for patrons to support the business and advertise to the public in style. Sticking with the ocean theme, typefaces similar to those used on water vessels were chosen for the logotype, business card, and menu. The menu is meant to look similar to a chalk-board with food icons throughout and text in white against the gray background. Overall, all design pieces within the project are cohesive and designed to stay true to the theme.
