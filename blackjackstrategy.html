Blackjack Strategy

Blackjack Strategy is a single-page web-application used to practice playing Blackjack. It follows mathematically accepted book/chart rules that determine the proper decision to make based on the dealer face-card and hand you are dealt as the player. I produced the Logo, Layout, Design, and functionality of the application. The objective was to design and develop an application that was simple, educational and fun.

VISIT APP

Blackjack Strategy

Visual Identity

To bring the casino-like look to the user, creating a design with standard casino cards and table colors was key. Hand signals are commonplace at a Blackjack table, so I wanted to incorporate hand icons in some way. Given that there is always one "correct" move to make for a player to have the best probability of winning a hand, it was important to also clearly indicate what that "correct" move is.

Application Guidelines

The multi-layered logo features a Blackjack hand of Ace/King sitting atop three black chips. With a Blackjack being the best possible hand, it only made sense to use it in the logomark. Depicting the game's nickname, A large "21" is placed behind the cards and chips. The logotype reading "Blackjack" with a subscript reading "Strategy" is centered over the logomark to form an all-encompassing logo.


I selected "Prompt" for buttons and short descriptions explaining "correct" and "incorrect" responses throughout the app. Its wide appearance does a nice job making small amounts of text fill open space. "Bright DEMO" was chosen to bring a traditional casino feel to the app. The mixture of thick and thin strokes with rounded, soft serifs epitomizes the old-school vibe. The color scheme is true to those typically seen in casinos. An array of greens are used for different table felts, while red is a nice complementary color that is found on casino cards, chips, etc.

Wireframe

When putting together the wireframe, I outlined a Dealer vs. Player interface that would make it clear what the concept of the app was. The focus was to be placed on the center of the page, with game-type toggle buttons at the top, and action buttons lining the bottom. When an action button is selected, a "correct" or "incorrect" message would appear, and the action buttons would turn into a button that when chosen, presents the user with a new hand.

Prototype & Demo

I created buttons for both single and multi-deck games. This allows the user to have the option of playing either and to develop skills for both. The game of Blackjack is played against the dealer, so the interface was designed to clearly depict two sides: the competition across the table, and the player. The four buttons reading "Hit", "Stand", "Double", and "Split" are nicely labeled and include icons that demonstrate the associated hand motion that should be made while sitting at the table. Once one of those four buttons are selected, an informative message appears, letting the user know if their selection was correct or not, and why. Simultaneously, a "New Hand" button appears, giving the user a convenient way to play another hand. The limited navigation leads to simple decision-making and a rewarding experience.

Complete Application & Demo

The end product is almost identical to the prototype from both a visual and operational perspective. Minor differences in button and font size make up the differences.
