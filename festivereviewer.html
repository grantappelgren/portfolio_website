Festive Reviewer

Festive Reviewer is a blog website where I share my experiences attending different music festivals. The intended audience is any festival-goer interested in learning information, pros, cons, etc. about these events.

VISIT WEBSITE

Festive Reviewer

Visual Identity

I wanted to design a very straightforward interface that did not interfere with the subject-matter. Blogs should have the ability to be added upon, so a format that would be able to easily adjust as new content is added was important. Simple navigation and a consistent way of displaying my reviews of each festival across pages would help users compare festivals to one another.

Typography

"Michroma's" short and wide square-like appearance gives it a futuristic look, making it perfect for titles and headers. It works well with "Montserrat Light", which is a very thin and delicate typeface used for the website's body.

Neutral, light gray and dark blue make up the majority of text and white space, while tints of green and orange are used to semantically represent past (green) and future (orange) festivals as well as their strong (green) and poor (orange) grades. Dark red is used sparingly for a gradient effect.

Logo

When connecting a circle to the left side of the "F's" base, the letter itself becomes the stem and flags of a sixteenth music note. Likewise, the circle portion of the sixteenth note acts as the head of a person with the "V" doubling as the their collar. In this case, the person is me, the reviewer.

Landing Page Wireframes

A slideshow of festival images graces the top of the landing page, immediately offering users a glimpse into the festival atmospheres. Festivals are listed as buttons, providing a way for users to interact with the site and access individual festival pages. On each of these pages, the same four columns (Production, Variety, Atmosphere, Return?) would be featured and assigned letter or Yes/No grades, developing a way to rank festivals by section. Simple nav-bar and footer layouts with links and clickable icons border the top and bottom of each page.

Complete Landing Page & Website Demo

The final website successfully brings the wireframes to life. Minor changes from the wireframes to the website include a left-aligned nav-bar and minimalistic footer containing three centered and evenly-spaced contact links.
