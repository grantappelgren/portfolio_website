Weather Check

Weather Check is a single-page web-application used to check the weather. It is powered by the Open Weather Map API. The objective was to design and develop a tool with an inviting interface that is user-friendly. Certain elements such as mobile app icon and splash page were designed in case a native or hybrid mobile-version was to be built in the future.

VISIT APP

Weather Check

Visual Identity

Weather has a look and feel, so I wanted to incorporate the current weather into the background to give the user a sense of being outside. Checking different locations and toggling temperature settings is commonplace, so ensuring the interactive aspects of the app were enjoyable was also a point of emphasis.

Application Guidelines

To ensure people would immediately recognize the logo as pertaining to weather, I decided to use a thermometer connecting sun and snowflake icons to represent high and low temperatures.


"BIGNOODLETITLING" was specifically chosen for the logotype, being a tall and bold typeface that pairs nicely with the vertical nature of the logomark. I chose the clean and simple "Roboto" typeface, which would not be a distraction from the content. The color scheme consists of a wide range of warm and cool colors to cover the potential weather conditions on both sides of the hot/cold and day/night spectrums. When used together, they create beautiful gradients that effectively generate sky displays during different conditions.

Mobile App Icon

The logomark fits well within the square mobile-icon borders and is used here with a vertically-gradated background likened to the sky.

Splash Page

Inspired by mobile-applications such as Bleacher Report among others, I include the app logo and a recognition/"powered by" statement. In many cases the statement recognizes a sponsor, but here it made sense to give credit to the information source of the Open Weather Map API.

Weather Icons

Creating icons for different weather conditions was a matter of consistency. For instance, the sun, moon, clouds, and rain have the same exact design wherever they appear throughout the icon set. The minimalistic designs use basic shapes and recognizable weather identifiers with the purpose of utilizing familiarity. With slight alterations from icon-to-icon, a plethora of conditions are able to be represented.

Prototype & Demo (Save "Cairo, EG" as home)

A search bar used to change the location, as well as the current location are shown in large text, highlighting the importance of both elements. Beneath the location is an icon for the current weather condition, along with a description of said condition. To prevent a cluttered screen, I made sure to include only commonly-used weather information. Staying true to the most general measurement of weather being temperature, current temperature is front-and-center with high and low bounds below. The six-day forecast consists of columns providing the user with a condition icon and the predicted high and low temperature for each day. An imperial-to-metric degree toggle button allows users to customize the tool with ease. The background is ever-changing, displaying a gradient consistent with the current weather condition. A nav-bar sits at the bottom of the screen, giving users the ability to check the weather at their current or home location, set a new home location, or refresh the page.

Complete Application & Demo

I successfully replicated the prototype during production, with the functionality and UX developed as intended. Only slight changes to text styling and the nav-bar were made.
