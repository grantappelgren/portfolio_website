SUMAYYAH'S REFUGE

About the Project

Sumayyah's Refuge is a non-profit organization focused on serving the center-city Philadelphia region by assisting with community and economic development. Their mission is "to promote and provide meaningful connections through programs to aid in building healthy spirits, minds and bodies for marginalized communities, low-income individuals, and under-represented groups". As a completely new organization, they were looking for somebody to help develop a logo and visual brand identity. I partnered with them, creating logo, business card, and letterhead designs. The objective was to provide the necessary design material to lay a branding foundation that would be used to define a visual identity and expand their reach to donors, partners, volunteers, and those in need.

Visual Identity & Mood Board

Philadelphia's nickname is "The City of Brotherly Love". Sumayyah's Refuge follows that mantra, spreading love by lending a helping hand to those in need. With that, it was important to focus on the themes of love, togetherness, and growth. Incorporating symbolism to represent those themes throughout the logo, business card, and letterhead designs was imperative. Additionally, given that the designs were for a non-profit, I wanted to ensure that the overall look and feel of the visual brand identity generated a sense of safety, friendliness, and being welcome.

Typography

Looking for a sophisticated typeface to be used for the logotype, "Infinite Stroke" was selected. Its swooping lines and cursive style gives off a classic look. "Playfair Display" was chosen for its clean, professional aesthetic and to be used for business card and letterhead text.

The client's sole request for the color scheme was that purple needed to be featured. To represent love and growth, I decided to complement the purple colors with red and greens respectively.

Logo

The logomark consists of interweaving arms with hands ultimately coming together to form a heart, fitting the themes of love and togetherness. The arms are also designed to act as the trunk of a tree, which is a symbol of the third theme, growth. Leaves are placed around the hands to further depict the concept of a tree. When looked at as a whole, the leaves' circular nature also draws the eye to heart at the center of the design. The typeface sits just beneath the center of the logomark to round out the full logo.

Business Card

I wanted the business card design to be classy. To achieve this, I cut the colors used down to just the purples from the color scheme and added white. Using just those three colors, I was able to create soft gradients that contrast nicely with the sharp text. The shapes used throughout the design are meant to be abstract, but also overlap in a circular pattern to further exemplify togetherness. White font is used for just the cardholder's first name to stand out from the other text, while an all-black version of the logo is used in order to prevent distraction from the overall design. Everything is center-aligned, which makes for a balanced and legible presentation.

Letterhead

For communication purposes, the organization needed to have a professional letterhead template that could be used for on-brand correspondence. The design was to be consistent with that of the business card, making it clear who the written material is being sent from without even reading the content. Using the same abstract shapes and colors, I arrived at an elegant design that properly represents Sumayyah's Refuge. There is enough white space for a significant body of text, with shapes purposely formed around that space in a manner likened to a border. This template can be used for purposes such as reaching out to other organizations for partnerships or applying for grants with confidence.
