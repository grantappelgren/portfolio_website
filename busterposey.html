BUSTER POSEY

About the Project

Buster Posey is a retired Major League Baseball player. In 2011, he suffered a horrific season-ending injury that many thought would ruin his career. However, in 2012, he came back stronger than ever, and had one of the most remarkable seasons of all time. To pay tribute to this great season, I designed an infographic containing his statistics and awards won from the campaign.

ROLE

Design

CLIENT

Sports Content Orgs.

DATE

Nov. 2022

TOOLS

InDesign, Photoshop

Visual Identity

I wanted to represent numerical data in multiple ways, both literally and figuratively. At the same time, there needed to be a sense of balance, to avoid certain areas of information from being ignored. I intended to implement hierarchy, proper use of direction and line, and symbolism to make for an easy to understand graphic with meaningful shapes and icons. Baseball has a statistic for everything, so it was important to narrow down which ones to include to prevent an information-overload. With acronyms aplenty, the infographic is meant to appeal to an audience with basic understanding and exposure to basic baseball terms and statistics.

Typography

The San Francisco Giants, who Posey played his whole career for, use a very distinct typeface throughout their logo, logotype, jersey names, and numbers. I decided to use the same typeface for the infographic, to represent his ties to the team. It offers consistency and repetition, matching the text "SAN FRANCISCO" stitched across the chest of his jersey in the image at the center of the piece.

Colors

#FFFFFF

#EACCBA

#EE3524

#000000

Like the typeface, the color scheme is familiar and aligned with the Giants' organization. When used together, the four colors allow for fantastic contrast, perfect for highlighting and making information stand out on the page.

Infographic

The infographic starts with a large pointed shape containing the title and sub-title of the material. This shape represents home plate, where Posey would look down at and squat behind during almost every game of his career, playing catcher. By starting out the design centered in this way, I continue with the alignment by placing an image of the player in the middle of the page, following-through with his swing. This leaves space on both the left and right sides of the image, where I separate large statistical numbers and categories displayed in a generic sense on the left from bar-graph representations of first and second-half splits of the same categories on the right. Both sides are connected with a horizontal bar giving the piece nice symmetrical balance. The design is rounded out at the bottom with a list of accolades accompanied by baseball icons that symbolize the number of awards earned. A background of dirt to match the area surrounding home plate works well as a textured background for the entire page. Overall, the infographic meets the goal of being informational, symbolic, and easy to comprehend for the intended audience.
