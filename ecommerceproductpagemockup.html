E-COMMERCE

About the Project

E-commerce is a web-application product-page mockup for a camera company's online store. For a case study assigned as part of a job-application, I created a mockup for a product-page of a camera company's website. The mockup was required to contain sections for a navigation menu, five hero images, an item description, customer reviews, shipping and warranty information, an option to subscribe to the company newsletter, and a footer. The design needed to be unique, but not too complex for developers to produce.

Visual Identity

The photography industry is generally classy and professional, with cameras and associated equipment being extremely fragile and nuanced. To align with this, I wanted the page design to be clean, sharp, and serious. Realizing that there would be a significant amount of text spread throughout the page, applying balance, separation, and visual hierarchy was going to be important. Rather than displaying product images in a basic static format, I viewed this as an opportunity to incorporate interactivity, movement, and further implement an elegant showcase look-and-feel to the page.

Typography

I selected FORQUE, wanting a bold typeface with a bit of flair for the company logotype. With rounded apexes, ears, and tails, along with bars intersecting stems beneath their mid-points, flair is ever-present. For contrast, I wanted to use a thin san serif typeface for the page's content. Biko evenly displays characters, making it extremely balanced and perfect when using a significant amount of text. It pairs nicely with FORQUE, sharing similar round qualities throughout its anatomy, thereby adding a uniqueness to the page without taking away from the intended professional aesthetic.

Colors

#6B6B6B

#F3F3F3

#5879D0

To foster a very calm look, the color scheme purposely consists of only a few light colors, including a soft blue used to highlight buttons and certain small design elements. With the majority of cameras being black, it was important not to pull the user's attention away from the product.

Wireframes

A split 2-column design that condenses down to 1-column as the user's screen size decreases allows for a balanced page. This adjustment caters to different device widths, creating balanced designs that display the same content and economically use the alloted space. A top-aligned nav-bar and bottom-aligned footer house necessary links to redirect users to relevant pages. In both formats, the product name is centered above all other content, which is strategically placed below or adjacent based on importance. A rotating carousel for product hero images brings animation and smooth consistency that is reminiscent of an automobile show-room display. To give depth to the carousel, image opacity decreases as it moves further away. Horizontal lines are placed beneath section titles to create separation and consistency. The only major difference from the mobile to desktop design is the location of price on the page. It was moved beneath the carousel in the desktop design to push the images up, thereby adding more balance to the page.

Mockups

The product-page mockup effectively meets the case study specifications and provides a nice foundation that other pages of the web-application can follow to create a cohesive online shopping experience.
